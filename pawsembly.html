General Pawsembly

What's General Pawsembly About?

General Pawsembly is a pet and breeding simulator developed with Node and Express. A user can login with Google authentication and be allowed to create dogs and interact with the dogs they have created. The user is tasked with caring for the dogs and ideally breeding dogs together to get healthier dogs.

With General Pawsembly, you get the chance to create dogs from several different breeds. Every dog created has randomly generated stats for its brain, heart, lung, joint, and coat health. When a mixed dog is created the percentage and breeds of other dogs integrated with that pup is also randomly generated. What differentiates General Pawsembly from other pet and breeding simulators is that we allow mixed breeding. This provides a somewhat more realistic aspect as dogs are sometimes outcrossed to improve health statistics.

Random creation is not the only way to get a new dog. If you own a male and female dog, you can breed the two together to get a litter of puppies. The puppies breed mix will be dependent on that of the parents, and their health stats are based on Punnett squares of the parent's genetics. There is a lot to explore with this game, even in it's unfinished state.

Why make a game like this?

I love dogs and genetics. Since I was a child I have been obsessed with learning about different dog breeds. The first time Punnett squares were taught in my science class, I could not stop thinking about how it applied to dogs. In that regard, this is a passion project of mine.

It is great practice with APIs. I used two APIs in this project to collect the random dog images based on breed and dog breed information. However the APIs reference the dog breeds differently which provided a unique challenge to cross reference the APIs.

Biggest Challenges?

Cross referrencing dog breeds between the two APIs. Because the APIs referenced the dogs differently, it was challenging to map the two together. I managed to map it well enough to get a few breeds together to demonstrate the functionality of the app, but ideally I plan on going back to figure out a better mapping system.

Figuring out the best way to implement genetic health in the dogs. Behind the scenes this is actually being determined by a series of capital and lower case letters. It includes five sets of two letters. The health for that category is determined by the number of lower case letters present. Eventually I may allow players to have their dogs genetically mapped to expose this data, but for now players can only see the health of a category by general descriptors such as "Poor" or "Good".

Determining how to make health matter. This is a challenge I am still working on. I have considered various avenues as to how to handle it, but I did not have time to implement anything relating to this in the time frame I developed this project in.
